How SignVault works

A short, plain-language guide to what this website does — no technical knowledge needed.

What is it?

SignVault is a website where you put an unforgeable digital stamp on your documents — and the stamp carries your name, like “Signed by Anas.” After a file is stamped, anyone can check it in seconds, with no account needed:

1) who signed it, 2) that it is genuine, and 3) that it has not been changed even slightly since.

Think of a notary's stamp. A notary owns one special stamp that nobody else can copy. They press it on your document to prove it's official. Everyone recognises a real stamp, but no one can fake it. SignVault is that stamp — done digitally, by the website, on your files.

It works in three steps

Step 1

Upload

You log in and upload a document — a contract, a letter, any file.

Step 2

Sign

It is stamped in your name — the result reads “Signed by you,” and you get a share link.

Step 3

Verify

Anyone drops the file (or opens your link) — no login — and sees who signed it and Valid, or Invalid if edited.

What it guarantees

Nothing was changed. If even one letter of the document is altered after signing, the check turns Invalid.
You can see who signed it. The check shows the signer's name — and only this website can create a valid stamp, so a genuine result proves it.
There's a record. Every upload, signature and check is written to a log that can't be edited — so actions can't be denied later.

Behind the scenes, in plain words

The website holds a secret key that nobody else has — this is what makes its stamp impossible to fake. It also publishes a matching public "checker."

The secret key creates stamps. The public checker only confirms them. You can share the checker with the whole world safely — it can verify a stamp but can never reproduce one. That one-way relationship is the whole trick.
